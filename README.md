# BPMN-Project
# 📊 Project Summary: Business Process Analysis & Redesign – Bottles Wine Club

This project involved a comprehensive business process analysis and redesign for **Bottles Wine Club**, with the objective of identifying operational inefficiencies and proposing structured improvements to enhance overall performance. The work applied core principles of **Business Process Management (BPM)**, combining process modelling, qualitative analysis, and improvement methodologies to deliver a practical and scalable solution.

---

## 🔍 Project Scope and Approach

The project began with an in-depth analysis of the organisation’s existing (**“As-Is”**) business processes, particularly focusing on the **order fulfilment** and **membership management workflows**. These processes were mapped and evaluated to understand how activities were performed, how information flowed between roles, and where inefficiencies occurred.

To ensure a structured evaluation, the processes were assessed using the **Devil’s Quadrangle framework**, which examines four key dimensions:

- **Time** (process cycle time and delays)  
- **Cost** (resource utilisation and operational expense)  
- **Quality** (customer satisfaction and process accuracy)  
- **Flexibility** (ability to adapt to different scenarios)  

The analysis revealed several critical issues. The most significant inefficiency was **excessive waiting time**, particularly caused by delays in supplier responses and the dependency between membership approval and order processing. In some cases, the process cycle time extended from an estimated **2 days to up to 28 days** due to exceptions such as non-member orders and supplier unavailability.

Additionally, the process contained **redundant activities**, such as unnecessary forwarding of membership approvals, and lacked integration with supplier systems, resulting in manual effort and delays. These inefficiencies negatively impacted both operational performance and customer experience.

---

## ⚙️ Process Redesign and Improvements

Following the analysis, the project developed a redesigned (**“To-Be”**) process using **heuristic process redesign techniques** and **Lean methodology principles**. The focus was on eliminating non-value-adding activities, reducing waiting time, and improving workflow efficiency.

### Key Improvements

#### 1. Separation of Membership and Order Processes
The original process required customers to apply for membership after placing an order, creating significant delays. The redesigned process ensures that customers must complete membership approval **before placing an order**, removing dependency and reducing cycle time.

#### 2. Parallel Processing of Tasks
Previously, credit checks and application assessments were performed sequentially. The redesign introduces **parallel processing**, allowing these activities to occur simultaneously, significantly reducing processing time.

#### 3. Elimination of Redundant Activities
Non-value-adding steps, such as forwarding approved membership details between roles, were removed. This simplifies the workflow and reduces unnecessary processing effort.

#### 4. Supplier System Integration
A major improvement involves integrating supplier inventory systems with the company’s ordering platform. This enables:

- Real-time visibility of product availability  
- More accurate delivery timelines  
- Reduced need for manual supplier communication  

#### 5. Customer-Centric Process Design
The redesigned system allows customers to view inventory and delivery options directly, giving them more control and improving transparency.

---

## 🚀 Impact of the Project

The proposed improvements deliver significant operational and strategic impact across multiple dimensions:

### ⏱️ 1. Improved Efficiency and Reduced Cycle Time
By removing process dependencies and introducing parallel activities, the redesigned workflow substantially reduces delays. The separation of membership processing alone eliminates the potential **14-day delay**, resulting in faster service delivery.

### 📉 2. Reduction of Process Waste
Applying Lean principles helped identify and eliminate various forms of waste, particularly:

- Waiting time  
- Unnecessary task handovers  
- Redundant processing steps  

This leads to a more streamlined and efficient operation.

### 😊 3. Enhanced Customer Experience
The redesigned process improves customer satisfaction by:

- Reducing order processing time  
- Providing transparency in product availability and delivery  
- Minimising unexpected delays  

Customers benefit from a smoother and more predictable service journey.

### 🔄 4. Increased Flexibility and Scalability
The introduction of parallel processing and system integration increases the organisation’s ability to handle higher volumes of orders and adapt to changing demand. The system becomes more scalable and less dependent on manual intervention.

### 💡 5. Data-Driven Decision Making Potential
With supplier integration and improved process visibility, the organisation gains access to more accurate operational data. This creates opportunities for future enhancements such as:

- Process mining  
- Predictive analytics  
- Performance monitoring dashboards  

### ⚠️ 6. Consideration of Trade-offs
While the improvements offer clear benefits, the project also acknowledges potential challenges, including:

- Increased implementation costs (especially for system integration)  
- Reduced flexibility in certain areas (e.g., mandatory membership before ordering)  
- Risk of employee overutilisation if workload increases
However, the overall analysis concludes that the **long-term efficiency gains and customer benefits outweigh these drawbacks**.
